What is the Best Configuration for a Gaming PC?

To build the best Gaming PC, you must consider the configuration you are going with. There is no one size fits all answer to the question of what configuration makes the best Gaming PC. The best configuration for a Gaming PC depends on factors like your expected gaming resolution and how your system handles tasks. Lets take a brief look into the hardware factors that you should consider for the best configuration on your Gaming PC - 

Configure Your Gaming PC for Best Performance

CPU, GPU, RAM & Storage: To get the best gaming performance from it, make sure you are getting an overclockable processor with a higher base clock speed. Cache memory on the processor often plays a more important role than higher CPU core counts.

GPU or graphics card is considered the most important part of a gaming desktop PC. The GPU is responsible for processing visual data such as textures, ambient light, shadows, and details. The CPU, which in most Gaming PC builds does not feature an integrated graphics processing unit (iGPU). The CPU is also concerned with all the other jobs you command, unlike the GPU which only works with graphical data. In most cases, Star Tech has the most advanced Graphics Cards in Bangladesh from Nvidia, AMD, and the latest Intel Arc series.

Larger capacity, high-performing DDR5 Desktop RAM will hold more data in the system background for heavy programs to use, such as PC games. In todays scenario, you should get at least 8GB to 16GB RAM for moderate gaming. Get a higher frequency RAM kit like 3200MHz and beyond if you want smooth and faster loading times from your gaming desktop PC. Youll want to use an M.2 SSD at least for your boot drive to quickly access the files.

Motherboard & Power Supply: The next most important components for a gaming desktop PC  are the motherboard and power supply. While your motherboard works as a platform for all the components to communicate within them, the power supply delivers the right current through the motherboard. 80 Plus rated PSUs will deliver clean and stable energy to the mainboard. Things like overclocking, monitoring CPU temperatures, and customizing RGB are directly dependent on the motherboard. Ensure you are getting enough of the latest PCIe slots, M.2 & SATA III interface, and I/O ports on your motherboard to match your CPU, GPU, & SSD compatibility.

CPU Cooling and Casing: Choose a high-quality, rich heat exchanging CPU cooler so that the processor temperature remains in control. You can choose between a Liquid CPU Cooler and a CPU Air Cooler. Liquid-cooled Desktop gaming PC is more favorable and allows easy overclock temperature control and better heat removal. Gaming desktop PC casing also has a role to play in PC thermal management, and Gaming PC casings handle this job better. Gaming desktop cases come packed with the most optimized design for CPU cooling along with high-quality noise-reducing cooling fans and exclusive gaming RGB decorations.
